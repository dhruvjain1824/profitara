The Problem This Project Solves

Retail businesses sit on data they cannot read

Most small and mid-sized retailers export the same kind of file every month — a transaction CSV from their POS or e-commerce platform — and then do nothing with it beyond a total-sales number. Hidden inside that single file are answers to the five questions that actually determine profitability:

1. Where is money leaking?

Over-discounting, loss-making orders, and structurally unprofitable products quietly erase margin every month — and nobody can point to the exact dollar figure or cause.

2. Which customers are about to leave?

By the time a high-value customer is officially "lost," it's too late to win them back. Early warning needs to be automatic, not anecdotal.

3. What should be discounted, and by how much?

Discounting is usually a gut-feel decision. Without a profit-vs-discount model, businesses either overdiscount (losing margin) or underdiscount (losing sales).

4. Where should the business grow next?

Expansion decisions are often made on raw sales volume alone, missing markets that are smaller but far more profitable per customer.

5. How much stock is actually needed?

Without a demand forecast tied to inventory recommendations, businesses either tie up cash in excess stock or run out at the worst time.

Profitara's Answer

One CSV upload, fifteen analytical modules, zero spreadsheets. Every chart below ends in a recommendation with a dollar value attached — this is a decision-support tool, not just a report.

    ML FEATURE 4

    90-Day Profit Margin Forecast

    Prophet forecasts profit margin % for the next 90 days — not just revenue. Business use: if your revenue is growing but this forecast shows margin shrinking, you're growing unprofitably. Act before the quarter closes.

    ML FEATURE 5

    Customer Win-Back Probability (Logistic Regression)

    Among all churned customers, Logistic Regression scores each one by how likely they are to come back if contacted. Business use: stop wasting outreach budget on fully-lost customers — focus on the ones with highest win-back score first.

    ML FEATURE 6

    Inventory Turnover Risk Model

    Scores every sub-category by how risky its inventory position is — based on how fast it moves, how long it takes to ship, and how much discount it needs to sell. Business use: high-risk sub-categories need either faster reordering cycles or price adjustments.

    Demand Elasticity by Region

    Linear regression per region measures how much quantity sold changes when discount increases. Business use: an "Elastic" region responds well to discounts (sales jump), so targeted promotions there pay off. An "Inelastic" region doesn't — save the margin.
